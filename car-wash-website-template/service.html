Exterior washing

A surface cleaner with two to four high-pressure jets on a revolving bar that swivels when water is flowing is known as a pressure washer. The use of a high-pressure water spray to remove loose paint, mold, grime, dust, muck, chewing gum, and filth off surfaces and objects including buildings, automobiles, and concrete surfaces is known as pressure washing or power washing. A mechanical pressure washer's volume is measured in gallons or liters per minute and is usually fixed. The pump's pressure, which is measured in pounds per square inch, pascals, or bar, is fixed, although it may be adjusted by altering the unloader valve.
